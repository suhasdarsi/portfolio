I recently visited Vietnam and spent a good amount of time exploring bags—especially some lesser-known brands and shops that most people might not have heard of. During this trip, I checked out brands like Yuzu (more on that in a future post), along with a few other lesser-known names such as Fy Guys and Saigon Swagger.

But in this post, I want to focus specifically on Ctactical in Vietnam, because I actually went to their physical store—and before visiting, I noticed there was very little online (especially on Reddit) about what the shop experience is like. So here are my two cents.

## Location: Not Central, Not "Tourist Core," but Still Reachable

Ctactical's shop isn't located right in the center of the city. It's a bit removed from the classic landmarks like the cathedral or the post office area—roughly 8 km away from those famous spots.

## Getting There: Buses Worked Well for Me

If you're planning a visit, I'd suggest taking buses. I found Vietnam's bus system pretty easy to use for getting around.

That I did take a cab the first time I went—and unfortunately, I arrived to find the shop closed at 6:00 PM on a Sunday. So I had to return another day.

## What's In-Store: Some Sets of Bags, But Not Everything Online

When I visited again, the shop had some sets of bags, but not everything that's listed on the website. So it's worth setting expectations that the full online catalog may not be reflected in the physical store.

## Pricing: Lower In-Store Than Online (Even Lower Than Shopee)

One of the best reasons to visit in person: the prices were generally lower than on the website, and they were even lower than what their Shopee store offers. If you're already in Vietnam and considering a purchase, visiting the shop can be a solid deal.

## What They Had (and What I Didn't Love)

In-store, I saw:

- Their bags (including a sling)
- Some camping gear
- Pouches and other accessories
- Pencil cases (in two sizes)

Personally, I wasn't a fan of the sling's styling—it's clearly meant to be tactical, but it just isn't my cup of tea.

## What I Wish They Stocked

Even though they have a "one bag for everything" kind of feel, there were some items I really wished they had available in-store:

- A toiletry bag
- Packing cubes
- A packable bag

For me, a packable bag is especially important when traveling. I like using a sling, but I still feel that lugging a fairly large bag around the city isn't the most comfortable experience—so having a packable day bag option would be huge.

## Overall Impression

The store wasn't something to "write home about" in terms of having an incredible selection—but it was still a genuinely interesting visit, especially because it's something I'd planned to check out for a long time.
