*After months of daily use across multiple cities, here's what I actually think.*

---

I bought the Pakt Travel Backpack 3 because my previous GORUCK bag did the job, but it always made me look like I was headed to a war zone. I wanted something that still worked hard, but looked a bit more subtle. This is my review after using it as my primary "one bag" for business travel and everyday carry.

---

## First Impressions: Build Quality

The fit and finish are solid. The bag feels well built, the material feels premium, and nothing about it screams "cheap." It also blends in nicely and doesn't look as expensive as it is — which I actually like. I can easily see this lasting a long time, and the lifetime warranty helps with peace of mind.

---

## Feature Overload

That said, the bag has a *lot* of features, and I don't use half of them. I thought I would, but in real life I keep coming back to the same few compartments. If you love bags with a ton of options and "just in case" features, this bag will probably feel like a playground. If you're more of a simple, one-and-done person like me, it can feel like the bag is trying to do too much.

---

## What I Actually Like

**Load lifters.** My previous bag didn't have them, and I appreciate having them here.

**Grab handles.** Excellent — makes it easy to move the bag around quickly.

**Water bottle holder.** One of my favorite parts. On my GORUCK, the bottle pocket technically existed, but it ate into the internal volume in a way I hated. On the Pakt, it works better for how I pack.

One annoyance worth flagging: the material picks up lint. It makes the bag look older and less clean pretty quickly. If you care about keeping your bag looking fresh, expect to do some regular cleanup.

---

## The Front Pockets

Up front, you get two pockets.

The first one is the useful one — it has organization, a magnetic clasp, and an AirTag holder. I love the AirTag placement. I'm not even using it yet, but it's one of those details that feels thoughtfully done.

There's also a key leash, but it's plastic and feels flimsy. It might hold up fine, but I don't feel confident trusting it with anything important.

There's also a pen holder that I actually use. I tried using it for my Apple Pencil, but it falls off sometimes, so I'm still on the fence there. For a normal pen, though, it's great for quick access when I need to jot something down during work travel.

---

## The Main Compartments

Once you move into the main zips, the bag starts showing its "system" approach. There's an expansion area that gives you a bit more space (around a few extra liters). There's also a section with internal organization designed for Pakt's modular add-ons — you can loop things in, attach their internal pockets, and build a whole setup. It's well designed for people who like that. I don't. I'd rather keep it simple.

**The central compartment is where I spend 90% of my time, and it's the best part of the bag.** It opens clamshell-style, has plenty of usable space, and makes packing easy. When I travel, I mostly pack with cubes and use different sections for different clothing categories — I'll even separate clean and soiled clothes into different areas, which is genuinely convenient. I'm still figuring out the perfect layout, but the space makes it possible.

---

## Laptop & Device Management

The laptop compartment is placed in the middle of the bag, which is pretty unique. Functionally, it fits everything I've tried, including a 16-inch laptop. So size isn't the issue. My hesitation is more about structure and protection. I'm used to the GORUCK style where the laptop area adds rigidity. Here, the center placement is innovative and creates separation, but I'm still not fully convinced it's the best choice for how I like a bag to carry.

One thing I don't like: **there's no dedicated iPad slot.** I use my iPad regularly, and having to stack devices together in the same sleeve isn't ideal. It works, but for a bag at this price, I expected better device management.

---

## Travel-Specific Features

The bag includes a luggage pass-through with a magnetic setup — nice if you're pairing it with a roller. I usually travel with just this backpack, so it's not a feature I lean on much. Still, it's good to have and it's executed well.

One practical win worth calling out: **the height is perfect.** My GORUCK M23 used to give me trouble fitting into overhead storage on buses and similar spaces. This one avoids that issue entirely — a meaningful quality-of-life improvement.

---

## The Biggest Problem: Comfort

Now for the part that matters most to me.

The straps are fine — a bit firm, but I'm used to that. The real issue is the back panel and the way the bag sits. There's an "airport security" feature on the backside that I never use, and it seems to contribute to an awkward arch in the bag. That arch makes it less comfortable on my back and also affects how the bag stands on its own.

I've kept the hip belt attached even though I don't use it much, mostly because I'll probably lose it if I remove it. But even with or without the belt, I can't get past the back support. **For a bag that costs around $350, comfort should be nailed. Here it just isn't — at least not for me.**

---

## Weight

The bag is a bit on the heavier side. While I know newer Pakt bags exist, I'm judging this one based on what it delivers day-to-day. I don't regret buying it, and I do use it constantly — but I'm not sure I'd pay the same amount again without thinking hard about it.

---

## The GORUCK Comparison

What makes the comfort issue stand out even more is my experience with GORUCK. That bag is tough, military-looking, and not everyone finds it comfortable — but I did. I carried around 10+ kg for long distances across Southeast Asia and didn't feel much strain.

With the Pakt, I don't feel confident doing that kind of heavy, walk-all-day travel. Maybe it carries better when fully packed and structured, but based on how I use it, it's not there yet.

---

## Verdict

The Pakt Travel Backpack 3 is a well-built, feature-packed bag that looks good, blends in, and should last a long time.

If you love lots of compartments, modular systems, and travel features — you'll probably love it. But if comfort is your top priority, especially for long walks with real weight, the back support may be a deal-breaker.

In a future post, I'll likely do a dedicated review of my GORUCK bag and then compare the two directly. They're aiming at different vibes, but they overlap in the "one bag travel" world enough that a side-by-side breakdown feels useful.
