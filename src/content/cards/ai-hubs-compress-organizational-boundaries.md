Traditional software connects systems through explicit APIs and carefully designed workflows. AI can bridge them through language.

Give one assistant access to email, documents, calendars, financial data, and internal tools, and it becomes more than an interface. It becomes a hub through which previously separate systems begin to coordinate.

That compression is useful because complexity disappears for the user. It is also risky because boundaries disappear with it.

When many systems converge on one intelligent intermediary, its permissions, assumptions, and failures inherit the reach of everything connected to it.
