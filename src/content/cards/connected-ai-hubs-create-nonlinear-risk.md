One AI hub can concentrate access to many systems. Connecting that hub to another changes the shape of the risk.

A personal agent may talk to a company agent. The company agent may coordinate with financial, healthcare, or government systems. Each connection joins two already concentrated networks.

The resulting impact is not simply additive. A mistake can cross domains, gain new permissions, and return through a different path before anyone understands where it began.

We should evaluate agent connections less like ordinary integrations and more like links between critical infrastructure.
