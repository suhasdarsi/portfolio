Most software is designed to remove friction. With AI agents, removing every pause can become dangerous.

An agent may read sensitive data, make a decision, and trigger an action before a person can inspect the assumptions behind it. If the decision is wrong, seamlessness simply helps the error travel faster.

Confirmation steps, permission boundaries, spending limits, and human review can feel like product limitations. In an agentic system, they are security controls.

The safest AI experience may not be the one with the fewest clicks. It may be the one that knows exactly when to slow down.
